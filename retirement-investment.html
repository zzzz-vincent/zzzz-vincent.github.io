  • pre-tax 401k (Traditional 401k) ‚Üí contribute as pre-tax money, pay tax for contribution and growth when distribution. tax + 10% penalty for early withdrawing.
  • after-tax 401k (Some Company Offer) ‚Üí contribute as after-tax money. pay no tax for contribution but pay tax for growth when distribution. tax + 10% penalty for early withdrawing. After-tax 401k is not limited by individual deferral contribution limit but total 401(k) contribution limit.
  • Roth 401k ‚Üí contribute as after-tax money. pay no tax for contribution and growth when distribution. tax + 10% penalty for early withdrawing.
  • Roth IRA ‚Üí individual retirement account. contribute as after-tax money or mega backdoor conversion. pay no tax for contribution and growth when distribution. tax + 10% penalty for early withdrawing. Max cap per year ($6,500 for 2023). Not able to contribute if total income above certain amount ($83,000 in 2023).
  • Traditional IRA ‚Üí individual retirement account. contribute as pre-tax money, pay tax for contribution and growth when distribution. tax + 10% penalty for early withdrawing.

Total 401(k) Contribution Limit

  • total 401(k) contribution limit = max individual elective deferral 401k contribution (pre-tax 401k or Roth 401k) + company contribution (company match) + individual after-tax contribution (after-tax 401k)
    • eg. in 2023. total 401(k) contribution limit is $66,000. And pre-tax contribution limit is $22,500.
    • if your company match 50% of your pre-tax 401k. This means by maximum, you can contribute 66,000 - 22,500 - 11,250 = 32,250 from your after-tax income. This money will be saved in after-tax 401k account.

IRA contribution Limit

  • applied to both Traditional IRA and Roth IRA as a total limit
    • eg. 6,500 for 2023

Distribution: How to withdraw money

  • 401k and Traditional IRA: After 60, eligible to withdraw money from 401k and IRA with no penalty. Otherwise, 10% penalty.
  • Roth IRA: free to withdraw contribution part at any time. Withdraw earning part limited to 1) 60-year rule and 2) 5-year rule to avoid 10% penalty.
  • Loan from 401k account ‚Üí not recommended. Double tax for interest you pay yourselves.
  • Some other approaches such as disability, medical expense, etc.

Roth 401k and Roth IRA

  • Roth IRA contribution (not growth) can withdraw at anytime, no restriction. Roth 401k have 10% penalty before 60.
  • Roth IRA just like a normal brokerage account. Roth 401k have low cost mutual fund options.
    • tips: prefer low low cost mutual fund for retirement account.

Pro-Rata Rule

If a participant‚Äôs account balance in a plan qualified under ¬ß 401(a) or in a ¬ß 403(b) plan includes both after-tax and pretax amounts, then, under ¬ß 72(e)(8), each distribution from the account(other than a distribution that is paid as part of an annuity, which is subject to a different rule) will include a pro rata share of both after-tax and pretax amounts.
The Pro-Rata Rule is used to determine how tax-deferred money should be taxed upon withdrawal. Since a Backdoor Roth conversion involves withdrawing Traditional IRA funds and transferring them to a Roth IRA, the Pro-Rata rule applies.
This rule means that if you have mixed of pre-tax and after-tax money in Traditional IRA account. Then, when you withdraw (rollover to Roth IRA), the amount includes both pre-tax and after-tax proportionally.
Eg. I have 9,000 pre-tax and 1,000 after-tax in Traditional IRA. Even i only rollover 1,000 to my Roth IRA. I have 90% taxable. And my Traditional IRA has 8,100 pre-tax and 900 after-tax.
Note: the amount of pre-tax and after-tax is calculated by year. So you cannot roll-over 1,000 after-tax from Traditional IRA in Day1 and then put 9,000 pre-tax into Traditional IRA in Day2. From IRS perspective, in this year, you did the same as the above and will have 90% taxable.

Backdoor Roth: Traditional IRA ‚Üí Roth IRA

  • Requirement:
    • Limited by IRA contribution ($6,500 for 2023)
  • Steps:
      1. make a non-deductible contribution to a traditional IRA and then quickly convert it to a Roth IRA.
      1. Speed is an important element as you will owe tax on any investment earnings. No earnings, no additional taxation.
      1. only works if you don‚Äôt have any other IRAs (or 0 in other IRA accounts), otherwise Pro-Rata Rule may apply. ‚Üí Form 8606 Line 6 should be 0
      1. double check and file form 8606 in tax season
  • Double check following sections in tax form
    • Form 8606 Part I line 15c should be 0
    • Form 8606 Part II line 18 should be 0
    • Form 1040 line 15b should be 0

Mega Backdoor Roth: After-tax 401K ‚Üí Roth IRA

Double check with HR of the company before contributing to after-tax 401k. After-tax 401k is not a very good retirement investment. You should rollover it to Roth 401k or Roth IRA ASAP.
  • Requirement:
      1. employer allows after-tax option
      1. permit in-plan rollovers to a dedicated Roth account (a Roth 401(k)) or allow in-service non-hardship withdrawals
      1. Separate plan accounting of pre-tax vs after-tax contributions. Otherwise, Pro-Rata Rule applied
      1. Limited by total 401(k) contribution limit
  • Steps:
      1. max-out pre-tax 401k (not necessary but recommended)
      1. check employer‚Äôs matching
      1. contribute to after-tax 401k. The amount is equal to ‚Äútotal 401(k) contribution limit - pre-tax 401k - employer‚Äôs match‚Äù
      1. elect an in-plan Roth 401(k) rollover/conversion (if your plan has one and allows this) or you can roll the after-tax contributions into a Roth IRA, paying tax on the earnings. Confirm with company if this is allowed. Policies are various.
  • Pros and cons
    • Pros:
      • great increase for retirement account
      • gain benefits of Roth IRA ‚Üí no tax for growth
    • Cons:
      • limited flexible money for individuals
      • not allowed at company level

Use Roth IRA as Alternative for 529 Plan

  • if not a lot of children, eg. only one. Use Roth IRA as 529 is possible
  • Pros:
    • same as 529: tax-free for contribution distribution and earning
    • flexibility: can be used for retirement as well
